Product and brand development

Innovation toolkit

As the project manager and designer for a new White House innovation toolkit in support of GSA's Challenge.gov program, I worked to synthesize mounds of content into a usable training resource for innovators in the federal government. I led a 7-month kickoff team of three people, coordinated usability tests, drew up site concepts, and designed the brand presence. I interviewed managers and community to decompose stories into useful requirements and personas that acutely aligned to core users. Drafted user research reports to brief the team on needs and goals. Presented to applicable White House staff. Created all brand graphics and site design. Kept team on schedule and developed broad goals and requirements based on $0 budget and limited staff.

With an expanded 15-member team, I led content strategy and design refinements and worked with accessibility specialists to refine the brand and UX guide.

Consumer Stories

Debt collection is a sensitive topic, but people have rights when it comes to how they are treated when facing financial hardships. In order to raise awareness of the resources available to people at the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, I worked with various UX leads, media specialists, and developers to create and reorganize the suite of consumer videos, create the first model for money topic resources in the Consumer Experience Information Architecture project lead, and worked with video specialists to produce four new consumer interview stories.

I also worked with Bureau stakeholders to incorporate data from the CFPB research report: Consumer Experiences With Debt Collection into the story pages, as well as related action steps, and a call to readers to share their experience. This combination of data, resources, and personal stories better communicates the broader example of how this affects people from all walks of life.
